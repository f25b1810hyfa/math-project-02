Oil Palm FFB Growth & Yield Optimization

Utilizing Calculus to pinpoint optimal harvest windows, maximizing Malaysian crude palm oil (CPO) efficiency.

🌱 The Bioscience Scenario

In Malaysia, managing Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) maturity is vital for sustainable food security. If harvested too early, oil content is low; if harvested too late, free fatty acids ruin the oil quality. By tracking FFB weight over a 22-week development cycle, we model growth using a Logistic Growth Function. The derivative represents the Instantaneous Oil Accumulation Rate (kg/week).

Data Reference Framework: Inspired by growth curves published in the MPOB (Malaysian Palm Oil Board) Journal of Oil Palm Research.

🧮 Mathematical Model

Growth Function f(x)
f(x) = L / (1 + e^(-k(x - x₀)))

Where Max Weight (L) = 25kg, Growth Rate (k) = 0.4, Midpoint (x₀) = 12 weeks.

Derivative f'(x) [Rate of Change]
f'(x) = k · f(x) · (1 - f(x)/L)

Calculates exactly how many kilograms of biomass/oil the fruit is packing on per week.
